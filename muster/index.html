What "presumptive" means

For certain conditions, VA automatically assumes ("presumes") that your service caused your condition. Per VA.gov: "You don't need to prove that your service caused the condition. You only need to meet the service requirements for the presumption." No nexus evidence needed — just proof of qualifying service (the dates and places below).

Know the system

How "VA math" works

Ratings combine, they don't add. 50% + 30% isn't 80% — the 30% only applies to the 50% of you that's still "whole": 50 + (30% of the remaining 50) = 65%, which rounds to 70%. More disabilities means diminishing returns. That's why getting to 100% combined is hard.

P&T — Permanent & Total

A 100% rating that VA considers permanent (won't improve) and total. Unlocks the biggest benefits: CHAMPVA for family, Chapter 35 DEA education for dependents, property-tax breaks (state-dependent), and usually no future re-exams. The full stack this unlocks is laid out in the 100% P&T Stack tab.

TDIU (Individual Unemployability)

If service-connected disabilities keep you from holding steady work, you can be paid at the 100% rate without a 100% rating. Usually needs one disability at 60%, or a combined 70% with one at 40%+ (form 21-8940). Schedular P&T and TDIU are not the same status — see the Guides article on working with a 100% rating for why that distinction matters.

Key forms

21-526EZ file/increase a disability claim
21-0966 Intent to File — locks your effective date, gives you a year to gather evidence
21-4138 statement in support (your own account)
21-4142 authorizes VA to get your private medical records
21-8940 TDIU claim

What wins a claim

Three legs: a current diagnosis, an in-service event/exposure, and a nexus — a medical opinion linking the two ("at least as likely as not"). A strong nexus letter and a complete C&P exam matter most.

A 100% Permanent & Total rating quietly unlocks a second stack of benefits on top of your monthly compensation — health coverage for the family, education for the kids, housing tax breaks, and a possible path to a funded business. CHAMPVA — health coverage for your familyFed

~$4k–12kper year

Because you're 100% P&T, your spouse and children qualify for CHAMPVA — coverage that can replace or supplement private insurance. Cost-sharing is modest: roughly a $50/person, $100/family annual deductible and a 25% cost share, with an annual catastrophic cap around $3,000. Dental is not included.

NoteCHAMPVA is a secondary payer and not every provider accepts it — confirm your doctors take it before dropping any coverage you already have. If anyone in the family is also a military retiree, see CHAMPVA vs TRICARE in Guides.
First stepEnroll your family with the VA Office of Community Care (CHAMPVA application, Form 10-10d). Have dependents' documents ready.

Chapter 35 (DEA) — education for your dependentsFed

~$160ktotal, family

Each dependent gets 36 months of education benefits — roughly $1,500+/month at full-time (a monthly stipend, not tuition). Across a spouse and two children that's on the order of $160,000. For eligibility arising on or after Aug 1, 2023, there's no delimiting (expiration) date.

Part II
The VR&E Self-Employment Playbook

Veteran Readiness & Employment (Chapter 31) can fund your business. Few veterans use the self-employment track — here's how.

If your service-connected condition makes traditional employment hard, VR&E (Chapter 31) can assign you a counselor, help you write a business plan, and fund startup costs — equipment, software, licenses, sometimes inventory — plus a monthly subsistence allowance while you build the plan.

Read this firstBeing 100% P&T does not disqualify you. Eligibility hinges on a service-connected disability that creates an employment barrier, plus the drive/skills to run a business. Note the basic 12-year eligibility window, measured from separation or the date you were first notified of your rating, whichever is later — past it you need a "serious employment handicap" extension. And if any of your 100% is TDIU, confirm with your VSO how self-employment income interacts with it before you start.

The 4 steps

  1. Apply. VA.gov → Veteran Readiness and Employment (Chapter 31) → file VA Form 28-1900. It takes minutes and it's free.
  2. Meet your VRC. You're assigned a Vocational Rehabilitation Counselor. Ask specifically for the Self-Employment track, and bring a one-page concept: what the business is, who it serves, and why your disability makes self-employment the right fit.
  3. Build the plan. Work the business plan with the VRC — they assess viability and fund the approved pieces. VA will often pay for an outside business-plan consultant, which is genuinely useful.
  4. Lean on your County VSO. They push the paperwork for a living and it costs you nothing. Use them at every step.
The #1 reason vets get deniedA vague "I want to be my own boss" with no numbers. Bring a real market, real financials, and a clear tie between your disability and why self-employment fits. Specific beats enthusiastic.
First step this monthFile VA Form 28-1900 today, then book your County VSO to start the property-tax, CHAMPVA, and DEA paperwork in the same visit — one trip, several benefits opened.

Guide

CHAMPVA vs TRICARE — what covers a 100% P&T veteran's family?

If you're a 100% P&T veteran (and not a military retiree), your spouse and kids generally qualify for CHAMPVA, not TRICARE. TRICARE is for retirees/active-duty families; CHAMPVA is the VA program for dependents of permanently & totally disabled veterans. CHAMPVA has a modest deductible (~$50/person, $100/family), a 25% cost share, and a ~$3,000 catastrophic cap — but not every provider accepts it, and it doesn't cover dental.

What to check

  • You can't usually have both — eligibility depends on your status (retiree vs. P&T-only).
  • CHAMPVA is a secondary payer and coordinates with other coverage/Medicare.
  • Confirm your family's doctors accept CHAMPVA before dropping any current plan.
  • Apply through the VA Office of Community Care with your dependents' documents.

The mistakes that cost veterans this benefit

  • Dropping a working plan before confirming CHAMPVA is accepted by your providers.
  • Assuming CHAMPVA includes dental (it doesn't).
  • Not enrolling the family at all because no one mentioned it.

Property tax exemptions for 100% P&T veterans, by state

Most states offer a property-tax break for 100%/P&T disabled veterans — but the amount and rules vary widely. Some states exempt the home entirely; others exempt a set dollar amount of assessed value (California exempts roughly $180,671, or ~$271,009 for low-income households, saving ~$2,000–3,000/year). You almost always must file a claim — it isn't automatic.

How to claim it

  • Confirm your state's exemption type (full exemption vs. fixed assessed-value amount).
  • File with your county assessor once the home is your principal residence.
  • Check for a higher low-income tier if your household income is under the cap.
  • Re-file if you move — the exemption follows the principal residence, not you automatically.

The mistakes that cost veterans this benefit

  • Assuming it applies automatically — most require an application.
  • Missing the low-income tier that nearly doubles the exemption in some states.
  • Not re-filing after a move or refinance.

Source: State veterans agencies & county assessor guidance, as of 2026. Figures change yearly — confirm with your County VSO.

Guide

Can you work with a 100% P&T rating?

Usually yes — if your 100% is schedular Permanent & Total. A schedular 100% has no earnings limit; you can work and earn freely. The exception is TDIU (Individual Unemployability), where earning above the substantially-gainful threshold can trigger a review. Know which one you have before you start a job or business.

The key distinction

  • Schedular 100% P&T: your combined rating reaches 100%. No work or income restriction.
  • TDIU: paid at the 100% rate because you can't hold substantially gainful employment — income can affect it.
  • Permanent & Total also means no scheduled re-examinations, so the rating itself is protected.
  • Check your award letter or ask your County VSO which basis your 100% rests on — for free.

The mistakes that cost veterans this benefit

  • Assuming all 100% ratings have the same work rules — TDIU is the trap.
  • Starting a business under TDIU without confirming the income interaction first.
  • Not getting the schedular-vs-TDIU answer in writing from a VSO before earning.
