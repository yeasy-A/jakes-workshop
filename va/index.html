What "presumptive" means

For certain conditions, VA automatically assumes ("presumes") that your service caused your condition. Per VA.gov: "You don't need to prove that your service caused the condition. You only need to meet the service requirements for the presumption." No nexus evidence needed — just proof of qualifying service (the dates and places below).

🔥 Burn pits & airborne hazards

Qualifying service in these locations (or the airspace above them):

  • On or after Sept 11, 2001: Afghanistan, Djibouti, Egypt, Jordan, Lebanon, Syria, Uzbekistan, Yemen
  • On or after Aug 2, 1990: Bahrain, Iraq, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Somalia, United Arab Emirates
Presumptive conditions (per VA.gov)▾

Cancers: brain, gastrointestinal (any type), glioblastoma, head (any type), kidney, lymphoma (any type), melanoma, neck (any type), pancreatic, reproductive (any type), respiratory (any type).

Chronic illnesses: asthma diagnosed after service, chronic bronchitis, COPD, chronic rhinitis, chronic sinusitis, constrictive/obliterative bronchiolitis, emphysema, granulomatous disease, interstitial lung disease, pleuritis, pulmonary fibrosis, sarcoidosis.

🍊 Agent Orange

Presumed exposure for service in (per VA.gov):

  • Republic of Vietnam, its inland waterways, or within 12 nautical miles offshore — Jan 9, 1962 – May 7, 1975
  • Any U.S. or Royal Thai base in Thailand — Jan 9, 1962 – June 30, 1976
  • Laos — Dec 1, 1965 – Sept 30, 1969
  • Cambodia at Mimot or Krek, Kampong Cham Province — Apr 16–30, 1969
  • Guam or American Samoa — Jan 9, 1962 – July 31, 1980
  • Johnston Atoll — Jan 1, 1972 – Sept 30, 1977
  • Korean DMZ — Sept 1, 1967 – Aug 31, 1971
Presumptive conditions (per VA.gov)▾

Cancers: bladder cancer, chronic B-cell leukemia, Hodgkin's disease, multiple myeloma, non-Hodgkin's lymphoma, prostate cancer, respiratory cancers (including lung cancer), certain soft-tissue sarcomas.

Other illnesses: AL amyloidosis, chloracne (or similar acneiform disease), type 2 diabetes, hypertension (high blood pressure), hypothyroidism, ischemic heart disease, MGUS, Parkinsonism, Parkinson's disease, early-onset peripheral neuropathy, porphyria cutanea tarda.

At least 30 days total of service at Camp Lejeune or MCAS New River between Aug 1, 1953 and Dec 31, 1987.

8 presumptive conditions: adult leukemia · aplastic anemia and other myelodysplastic syndromes · bladder cancer · kidney cancer · liver cancer · multiple myeloma · non-Hodgkin's lymphoma · Parkinson's disease.

🩺 Free toxic exposure screening

Per VA.gov: "Every Veteran enrolled in VA health care will receive an initial screening and a follow-up screening at least once every 5 years." Screenings cover burn pits, Gulf War exposures, Agent Orange, radiation, Camp Lejeune water, and other hazards.

How: contact your local VA health facility, ask at your next appointment, or schedule a telehealth screening through VA's VET-HOME.

📨 Filing — including previously denied claims

File a new claim online, by mail, in person, or with a free accredited representative (form 21-526EZ).

Denied before, presumptive now? Per VA.gov: "If we denied your disability claim in the past and we now consider your condition presumptive, you can submit a Supplemental Claim. We'll review your case again." That's form 20-0995 — and "you don't need to wait for us to contact you before you file."

Know the system

How "VA math" works

Ratings combine, they don't add. 50% + 30% isn't 80% — the 30% only applies to the 50% of you that's still "whole": 50 + (30% of the remaining 50) = 65%, which rounds to 70%. More disabilities = diminishing returns. That's why getting to 100% combined is hard.

P&T — Permanent & Total

A 100% rating that VA considers permanent (won't improve) and total. Unlocks the biggest benefits: CHAMPVA for family, Chapter 35 DEA education for dependents, property-tax breaks (state-dependent), and usually no future re-exams.

TDIU (Individual Unemployability)

If service-connected disabilities keep you from holding steady work, you can be paid at the 100% rate without a 100% rating. Usually needs one disability at 60%, or a combined 70% with one at 40%+ (form 21-8940).

Key forms

21-526EZ file/increase a disability claim
21-0966 Intent to File — locks your effective date, gives you a year to gather evidence
21-4138 statement in support (your own account)
21-4142 authorizes VA to get your private medical records
21-8940 TDIU claim

What wins a claim

Three legs: a current diagnosis, an in-service event/exposure, and a nexus — a medical opinion linking the two ("at least as likely as not"). A strong nexus letter and a complete C&P exam matter most.
